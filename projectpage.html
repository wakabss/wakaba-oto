My Projects

"Bushwick Murals: A Canvas Divided" is a documentary that delves into the evolving street art scene of Bushwick, a neighborhood in Brooklyn, New York. This film explores the dynamic intersection of art, culture, and community, highlighting the changes that gentrification has brought to this historically Puerto Rican neighborhood.

The documentary centers around the vibrant murals that have become synonymous with Bushwick's identity. Through interviews with local artists and residents, the film examines the impact of these artworks on the neighborhood. It challenges the viewer to consider the fine line between beatification and gentrification, questioning who benefits from the art that adorns the streets.

As the creator of this documentary, I focused on capturing the essence of Bushwick's unique character. My role involved researching the history and current state of the neighborhood, conducting and filming interviews with key figures, and capturing extensive footage of the murals and street life. I employed a variety of shots to create a visually engaging narrative, from wide-angle views of the streets to close-ups of the artworks and the people behind them. The documentary aims to offer a balanced perspective, showcasing the beauty of street art while acknowledging the complexities it brings to the community.
